ABOUT LANDREST

Become acquainted with the LANDREST framework

LANDREST is a land degradation model that can be used to spatially identify locations in an area of interest that are in risk of being degraded. It represents interactions between biophysical and socio-economic factors that contribute to degradation.

It was developed to run as an ArcGIS tool based on the ModelBuilder concepts. The general structure behind it is inspired by the CORINE model with variables derived using the USLE. It is flexible enough to accommodate more variables beyond the number and types it was initially designed to handle.

The LANDREST version 0.1 has been thoroughly tested using spatial data captured in Babati district, Tanzania. It offers the following products to address soil degradation; potential soil erosion risk (PSER), actual soil erosion risk (ASER) and soil erosion risk (SER) itself.

This tool may be used to assess impact on the erosion risk of all policy interventions affecting sustainable land management. Nonetheless, LANDREST does not contain specific measures to reduce the degradation risks, such as terracing, mulching and afforestation among others.

COPYRIGHT

LANDREST is a unique spatial model developed with an objective of identifying areas in need of restoration due to degradation. This version (v 0.1) was developed to run in an ArcGIS environment as a model. It can be downloaded and used by anyone as long as the author is credited. The tool is intended for use by individuals, governments, NGOs, researchers, communities, and others seeking to address issues related to land degradation.
